Swotinsky's Favorite Dive Sites

Bonaire

Bonaire is an island in the southern Caribean Sea. Many reefs in Bonaire are so close to the shore, you don't even need a boat to see them! Swotinsky's favorite dive site in Bonaire is "Karpata".

According to Buddy Dive Resort...

With as many as 60 officially listed dive sites on Bonaire and another 20 dive sites on Klein Bonaire, the island truly is a Divers Paradise! Bonaire’s sea abounds with beautiful marine life and a fascinating variety of multi-colored tropical fish, there is lots to see while diving on Bonaire. The sea is remarkably calm, and conditions are superb for both scuba diving and snorkeling. On average, while diving on Bonaire, the visibility exceeds 100 feet (30 meters) and our average water temperature is 80 degrees Fahrenheit (28c). The fringing reef begins close to the shore, usually, a short 30 feet (10 meters) swim or less, making Bonaire world-renowned for its ease of shore diving. Bonaire’s waters offer something special for every diver. Novice divers will appreciate the calm waters while diving in Bonaire, reefs that run parallel to shore, and minimal currents. These conditions make it an ideal location to really work on improving those buoyancy skills! The shallow drop-off area of the reef slope with an abundance of marine life allows for a maximum bottom time while giving new divers an opportunity to develop a level of comfort and confidence in their newly learned skills.1

Roatan

Roatan is one of the bay islands off the coast of Honduras. Hundreds of years ago, Roatan was a pirate haven. Now it is a world-class dive destination. Swotinsky's favorite dive site in Roatan is "Hole-In-The-Wall."

Cozumel

Cozumel is an island off the coast of Mexico. In addition to Cozumel's awesome ocean dive sites, scuba divers can also go cavern-diving in fresh water cenotes. Swotinsky's favorite dive site in Cozumel is "Palancar Caves"

Cocos Island

Cocos Island is located approximately 300 miles off the coast of Costa Rica. It is famous for huge sea life like hammerhead sharks. To dive Cocos Island, many divers spend a week on a live-aboard board. Swotinsky has never been to Cocos Island but he hopes to someday!

What Equipment Do You Need to Go Scuba Diving

  • Essential Items
    • Buoyancy Compensation Device (BCD)
    • Tank
    • Regulator / Octopus
    • Dive Computer
    • Mask/Snorkel
    • Fins/Booties
    • Wetsuit
    • Dive Weights
    • Safety / First Aid

  • Accessories
    • Underwater Camera
    • Mask Defogger
    • Gear Bag
    • Save-A-Dive Kit
    • Underwater Tablet
    • Tank Banger

    Remember to get trained, certified, and always dive with a buddy!

SAFETY FIRST - Before you you dive, check your buddy!

  1. Check your buddy's BCD.
  2. Check your buddy's Weights.
  3. Check your buddy's Releases.
  4. Check your buddy's Air.
  5. Give your buddy a final check before you descend underwater.
